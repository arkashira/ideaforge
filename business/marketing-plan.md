# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
**Primary Positioning Statement:**  
"IdeaForge: Your AI-powered partner for generating and validating software ideas, designed specifically for indie hackers and creators."

**Alternative Positioning Statements:**  
1. "Transform your creative spark into viable software concepts with IdeaForge's AI-driven ideation tool."
2. "Overcome the ideation bottleneck with IdeaForge: the AI tool that empowers indie creators to innovate effortlessly."
3. "Unlock your potential as an indie hacker with IdeaForge, the AI solution that generates and validates your best software ideas."

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
1. **Persona 1: Indie Developer - Alex**
   - **Daily Job:** Developing and launching small-scale software projects independently.
   - **Budget:** $50/month for tools and services that enhance productivity and creativity.

2. **Persona 2: Content Creator - Jamie**
   - **Daily Job:** Creating digital content and looking for software solutions to complement their brand.
   - **Budget:** $75/month for tools that help streamline idea generation and project management.

3. **Persona 3: Startup Founder - Sam**
   - **Daily Job:** Leading a small startup team, focusing on product development and market fit.
   - **Budget:** $150/month for tools that assist in ideation and product validation.

## Channels
1. **Content Marketing (Blog & SEO)**
   - **CAC Estimate:** $20 per acquired customer through organic traffic and lead magnets.

2. **Social Media Advertising (Facebook & Instagram)**
   - **CAC Estimate:** $50 per acquired customer through targeted ads focusing on indie creators and developers.

3. **Partnerships with Creator Platforms (e.g., Indie Hackers, Product Hunt)**
   - **CAC Estimate:** $30 per acquired customer through collaborative promotions and featured listings.

## Content Cadence
- **Week 1:** Launch blog post introducing IdeaForge and its benefits; share on social media.
- **Week 2:** Publish a case study of a successful indie hacker using AI for ideation; promote via email newsletter.
- **Week 3:** Create a video tutorial on how to use IdeaForge effectively; share on YouTube and social media.
- **Week 4:** Host a live Q&A session on social media to engage potential users and gather feedback.
- **Week 5:** Release an infographic on the ideation process and how AI can help; distribute across platforms.
- **Week 6:** Share user testimonials and success stories; encourage sharing on social media.
- **Week 7:** Publish an article on common ideation challenges faced by indie hackers; promote via email.
- **Week 8:** Create a downloadable guide on best practices for software ideation; use as a lead magnet.
- **Week 9:** Launch a podcast episode featuring interviews with successful indie creators; promote on social media.
- **Week 10:** Run a contest for users to submit their best ideas using IdeaForge; promote across channels.
- **Week 11:** Share a behind-the-scenes look at the development of IdeaForge; engage audience on social media.
- **Week 12:** Recap of the 90-day journey, highlighting key milestones and user feedback; promote via email.

## Launch Milestones
- **D-30:** Finalize product features and user interface; begin beta testing with select users.
- **D-0:** Official launch of IdeaForge; initiate marketing campaigns across all channels.
- **D+30:** Gather user feedback and iterate on product features; release first major update based on feedback.
- **D+90:** Evaluate performance against success metrics; plan for next phase of product development and marketing.

## Success Metrics
- **DAU (Daily Active Users):** Target of 1,000 DAU by D+90.
- **$MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) Target:** $10,000 in MRR by D+90.
